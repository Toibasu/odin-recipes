Okonomiyaki

A savoury Japanese pancake, made with cabbage and chunks of pork belly. Great way to use your leftovers for a nice and tasty meal.

Ingredients

Main

  • 1 egg
  • 60 plain flour
  • 1 tsp vegetable bouillon powder
  • 200g green cabbage, finely shredded
  • 100g cooked prok belly, thinly sliced into bite sized pieces

Sauce

  • 1½ tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1½ tbsp ketchup
  • 1½ tbsp caster sugar

Toppping

  • 2-4 tbsp mayonnaise
  • 1 green nori(seaweed) sheet, shredded
  • 3 spring onions, finely sliced
  • katsuobushi (bonito flakes), to serve(optional)

Method

  1. Crack the egg into a large bowl and tip in the flour, bouillon powder and 100ml water. Mix well. Stir in the shredded cabbage and season well.
  2. Heat the oil in a small, deep frying pan or skillet (ours was around 19cm) over a medium heat and fry the pork belly slices for 4-5 mins until golden and the fat has rendered. Pour the cabbage mixture over, stir everything together and fry for 5 mins until dark brown underneath and semi-cooked. Flip and cook for 5 mins more.
  3. Meanwhile, make the sauce by whisking all the ingredients. Season lightly.
  4. When the okonomiyaki is cooked, transfer to a serving plate and top with the sauce, mayo, green nori, spring onions and katsuobushi, if using.
